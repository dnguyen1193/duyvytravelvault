Extra spots & smart swaps

If you want to mix it up

You mentioned a few maybes. Here's my honest take on each, plus additions I think you'll love — with when they actually fit your days.

Henderson Waves Bridge

Swap into Dec 19 PM

Singapore's highest pedestrian bridge — sculptural wood, glowing with LEDs at dusk. Beautiful, but geographically awkward (near Mount Faber in the south) and only sings at blue hour.

◆ Best fit: near HarbourFront on the way back from Universal. Do it ~6:45pm instead of USS fireworks if a moody sunset bridge beats fireworks for you.

Haji Lane & Sultan Mosque

Add to Dec 20 or Dec 22 AM

Kampong Glam's hip alley of street art and boutiques, anchored by the golden-domed Sultan Mosque. Some of the most stylish, colourful couple backdrops in the city.

◆ Strongly recommend. Swap in for the National Museum on Dec 20, or trade Chinatown for it on Dec 22.

Old Hill Street Police Station

Built into Dec 18

The building with 927 rainbow-coloured shutters — a 2-min, high-impact photo stop on your Clarke Quay walk.

◆ Easy yes. Already on the route, costs nothing extra.

CHIJMES

Dinner alt · near hotel

A restored Gothic chapel and courtyard turned dining enclave, minutes from your hotel — lovely lit up at night.

◆ A pretty, convenient dinner option any evening near City Hall.

Koon Seng Road (Joo Chiat)

Half-day add-on only

Singapore's most photogenic Peranakan shophouses — a rainbow row that's pure couple-shoot gold. The catch: it's out east, a real detour.

◆ Worth it if you free up a morning; otherwise Chinatown + Haji Lane already cover that colour palette.

Clarke Quay bumboat ride

Dec 18 evening

A short river cruise past the quays and under the bridges, with the skyline opening up at the bay — relaxed and romantic for a first night.

◆ Nice add to arrival night if you're not too jet-lagged. Very low effort.

Good to know

Singapore know-how

The practical stuff that makes the trip frictionless.

🛂 SG Arrival Card

Mandatory. Submit free online via the ICA website/app within 3 days before you land. Do it for both of you.

📶 Connectivity

Buy a tourist eSIM in advance or a SIM at Changi (Singtel/StarHub/M1, ~S$12–30). Free Wi-Fi is widespread too.

🚇 Getting around

The MRT is fast, clean and cheap. Tap a contactless Visa/Mastercard in and out — no separate transit card needed. Use Grab for taxis.

💳 Money

Currency is the Singapore Dollar (S$). Cards almost everywhere; keep small cash for hawker stalls. Tipping isn't expected.

🔌 Power

Sockets are Type G (UK 3-pin square), 230V — different from Vietnam. Bring an adapter. Tap water is safe to drink.

🌧️ Weather

December is monsoon season: hot & humid (26–32°C) with frequent short downpours. Carry a compact umbrella; malls and museums are good rain backups.

📷 Camera in the climate

Humidity = condensation. Let gear acclimatise moving AC↔outdoors, carry a microfiber cloth and silica gel packs. Pack spare batteries + memory cards. Power banks in carry-on only.

⚖️ Local laws

Singapore is strict: vaping is illegal (heavy fines), as is littering, jaywalking, and eating/drinking on the MRT.
